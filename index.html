Block Pulse live

Every NYC DOT street camera, graded for whether it can see sidewalk life — and a live read of how alive each block is, scored by a vision model.

This watches a sliver of the city. NYC DOT runs ~960 cameras to manage traffic, not street life. They cover roughly 1% of the city's ~6,300 street-miles and about 7% of signalized intersections — clustered on arterials and bridges. Of the 744 on neighborhood streets, only 390 (≈40%) can actually see a sidewalk, and 87 of the 99 best are in Manhattan. The Bronx and Staten Island are almost all highway — effectively unwatched here.
